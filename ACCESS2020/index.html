Audio samples from "Sequence-to-Sequence Emotional Voice Conversion with Intensity and Duration Control"

Authors: Heejin Choi, Minsoo Hahn

Abstract: This paper proposes an improved emotional voice conversion (EVC) method with emotional intensity and utterance duration controllability. EVC methods without duration mapping produce emotional speech with identical duration to that of the neutral input speech. In reality, even the same sentence would have different duration and speed depending on the emotion. To solve this, our proposed method adopted a sequence-to-sequence network with an attention module that enables the network to learn attention in the neutral input sequence should be focused on which part of the emotional output sequence. In addition, to capture the multi-attribute aspects of emotional variations, an emotion encoder is designed for transforming acoustic features into emotion embedding vectors. By aggregating the emotion embedding vectors for each emotion, a representative vector for the target emotion is obtained and weighted to reflect emotion intensity. y introducing speaker indexing, the proposed method can preserve speaker identity fairly well even after the emotion conversion. Objective and subjective evaluation esults confirm that the proposed method is superior to other previous works. Especially, in emotion intensity control, we succeeded in getting fairly successful results.
