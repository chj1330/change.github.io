Audio samples from "Sequence-to-Sequence Emotional Voice Conversion with Intensity Control"

Authors: Heejin Choi, Minsoo Hahn

Abstract: This paper proposes an emotional voice conversion system capable of controlling emotional intensity levels. Emotional voice conversion methods without duration mapping produce emotional speech samples with lengths identical to those of the input neutral speech samples. In reality, the same sentence is uttered, just at different speeds depending on the emotion type. To solve this problem, the proposed method applied a sequence-to-sequence network with an attention module that enables the network to learn where attention should be focused in the input neutral sequence with regard to each item in the output emotional sequence. To capture the multi-attribute aspects of emotional variations, an emotion encoder is designed for transforming acoustic features into emotion-embedding vectors. By aggregating the emotion-embedding vectors, representative vectors in accordance with each target emotion are obtained and weighted. With an explicit speaker annotation, the proposed method maintains the identity of the speaker in the event of an emotional conversion. Objective and subjective evaluation results demonstrate the superiority of the proposed method relative to baseline methods. Specifically, results on emotion intensity recognition highlight the emotion strength controllability of the proposed method.
